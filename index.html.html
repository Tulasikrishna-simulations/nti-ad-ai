The problem

Ads are engineered to bypass your reasoning

Advertising systems optimize not just to persuade — but to suppress your capacity to recognize that you're being persuaded.

⚡

Stacked persuasion techniques

Scarcity, urgency, authority, and social proof deployed simultaneously. Their effects compound non-linearly — the combination is far stronger than any single technique.

🔥

Emotional state manipulation

Fear, desire, FOMO, and excitement are deliberately triggered to move viewers from deliberate analytical thinking into emotionally-driven impulsive response.

🧠

Cognitive load flooding

Fast cuts, dense overlays, simultaneous audio-visual stimulation. All consume processing bandwidth — reducing the cognitive resources available for critical evaluation.

👁

Fabricated authenticity

Visual geometry manipulation, AI-generated behavioral signals, and exaggerated physical claims create false credibility increasingly difficult to detect without computational help.

PAVE-C Framework

Five dimensions of advertising influence

Each dimension captures a distinct mechanism. Together they form a complete picture of how an advertisement operates on the viewer.

PI

Persuasion Intensity

Aggregate strength of psychological triggers — urgency, scarcity, authority, social proof, FOMO.

PI = 1 − ∏(1 − wᵢ·pᵢ)
EAI

Emotional Arousal Index

Degree to which fear, desire, guilt, and excitement are triggered to override rational decision-making.

EAI ∈ [0, 1]
CLF

Cognitive Load Factor

How much processing bandwidth the ad consumes, reducing capacity for critical evaluation. A new dimension absent from prior frameworks.

CLF ∈ [0, 1]
VI

Verifiability Index

Checkability and plausibility of claims, weighted by specificity. Quantitative claims are more verifiable — and more dangerous when false.

VI = Σ tᵢ·spec(cᵢ) / m
AS

Authenticity Score

Behavioral and visual consistency. Combines entropy-based motion analysis with geometric scale estimation to detect fabricated presence.

f(GSS, BUS, geo)
The formula

Three layers. One score.

The Transparency Score is computed through two intermediate values. The multiplicative structure is deliberate — emotional suppression amplifies deception, it does not merely add to it.

1

Cognitive Bypass Factor (CBF)

CBF = (EAI + CLF) / 2

How suppressed is the viewer's rational processing? High emotional arousal + high cognitive load creates conditions where even obvious manipulation passes undetected.

2

Deception Risk Score (DRS)

DRS = 0.40·PI + 0.35·(1−VI) + 0.25·(1−AS)

How inherently deceptive is the content, independent of viewer state? PI is the dominant factor (0.40), followed by unverifiable claims (0.35), then fabricated authenticity (0.25).

3

Transparency Score (TS)

TS = (1 − DRS) × (1 − CBF) × 100

If DRS = 0.7 and CBF = 0.8 → TS = 6 / 100. Not 10. The multiplication correctly models how cognitive suppression amplifies the effect of content deception — this is what makes it more dangerous than either factor alone.

Temporal extension: PI_eff = PI × [1 + τ · (1 − t_first / T)] — urgency appearing in the first 2 seconds of a 30-second ad amplifies PI by up to 1.47×. Manipulation is more dangerous when it arrives before the viewer has formed a critical stance.
