How it works

Behavioral Authenticity Metrics

The AS sub-layer combines geometric stability, behavioral entropy, and visual consistency signals into a single authenticity estimate.

Geometric Stability Score (GSS)

GSS = Var(θ(t))

Variance of movement angles over time. In images, approximated by facial landmark alignment variance. Extremely low variance indicates artificial smoothness (AI-generated). Natural humans show predictable micro-variation.

Behavioral Uncertainty Score (BUS)

BUS = −Σ p(x) log p(x)

Entropy of behavioral state distribution. Low entropy = artificial regularity. Natural human facial expressions have controlled unpredictability — not random, but not perfectly uniform either.

Facial Symmetry Index

FSI = 1 − |d_L − d_R| / max(d_L, d_R)

Compares distances from nose midline to left and right landmarks. Perfect symmetry in AI-generated faces often deviates subtly. Natural faces show slight asymmetry that feels organic.

Authenticity Score (AS)

AS = f(GSS, BUS, FSI, lighting)

Combined score ∈ [0, 1]. Used as the AS dimension in the full PAVE-C Transparency Score formula: DRS = 0.40·PI + 0.35·(1−VI) + 0.25·(1−AS). Low AS directly increases DRS and reduces TS.
